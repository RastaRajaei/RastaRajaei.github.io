WHO AM I

I am Rasta Rajaei…

People who watch the Breaking Bad series often ask me why I decided to quit chemistry. Yes, it was indeed a bold move 😃. I quit my Bachelor's degree in chemistry despite studying at one of my country's best institutions, Isfahan University of Technology(IUT), and having really good grades. Why did I quit? Well, the reason is simple: it just wasn't me.

That's when I discovered forestry. Amazingly, I had not even considered this major when I first entered the university. As a child, I had a keen interest in learning about different types of trees, soil materials, and anything related to nature. I even had collections of different tree materials. I spent numerous hours in the field, conducting simple experiments, painting from nature, and so on. In fact, I am a painter and pursue this hobby at a good level.

Upon having a fulfilling undergraduate experience in forestry, I decided to pursue a master's degree in forest genetics. I was accepted at TMU and completed my thesis under the supervision of Dr.Hamed Yousefzadeh. Those two years were probably some of the best times in my life; I was deeply engaged in something I loved doing. Alongside completing my master's degree, I also published two articles.

In 2022, I returned to the university after resolving personal problems. Initially, I had planned to continue my study in forest genetics. However, upon taking a biometry course with Dr.Hormuz Sohrabi who suggested that I undertake a simple class assignment on mapping tree species in mixed Hyrcanian forests using remote sensing and Python, I made a startling self-discovery. I found that I truly enjoyed the experience of the project and it perfectly matched my skills, given it involved working with software. It opened up a whole new, magical, and challenging world in front of me, a world I am eager to explore.

Let me clarify that I am not completely distancing myself from forest genetics. Who knows? Maybe one day, after becoming adept in remote sensing and programming, I will pursue a link between forest genetics and remote sensing.

I am currently privileged to collaborate with Dr. Hormoz Sohrabi ,Dr. Yago Diez and Dr. Omid Ghorbanzadeh on a research project titled "Individual Tree Detection, Crown Delineation, Species Classification, and Vital Assessment Using Deep Learning and UAV Imagery. " This project, identified by ID number 48497529, is being conducted in three phases. The outcomes of this research will undoubtedly contribute significantly to forest management efforts, particularly in afforestation initiatives. You can find more details about this project in the graphical abstract and abstract.

Title:

Individual Tree Detection, Crown Delineation, Species Classification, and Vital Assessment Using Deep Learning and UAV Images

Abstract:

In the effective management of natural forests and extensive afforestation, where classical mapping methods are practically challenging, time-consuming, laborious, and unstable, the need for a cost-effective automated approach is apparent. With the emergence of deep learning techniques, it is now possible not only to detect or delineate individual tree crowns but also to simultaneously detect tree bases, identify species, and monitor forest health. Meanwhile, optical Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV) data are useful for capturing vegetation characteristics due to the rich semantic information they provide. The upcoming research aims to use UAV images to explore the application of deep learning approaches for individual tree segmentation, detection, species classification, and health status assessment of individual tree crowns (in three categories: alive, declining, dead) in forests. The study areas, 1). once located within the Mobarakeh Steel Company(MSC) in southwest Esfahan, Esfahan Province, Iran, spans over 1500 hectares with cold dry climate according to the Amberger classification. This area includes afforested zones of mixed even-aged/uneven-aged broadleaf-conifer stands, where declining and dead trees are a concern for this forest management. 2). And the second study area is a dense mixed forest, named Noor forest, located in the north of Iran. This forest is 3682 ha in extent. 3). The other study area is a landscape of Chaharmahal-va-Bakhtiari province, Helen, containing Natural Pure Zagros forests that is 17 ha in extent. Aerial photography of the entire of all study areas is conducted using UAVs; additionally, part of the ground truth data is obtained through field inventory, and the other ground truth data is collected using remote sensing methods (visual interpretation). For example, approximately 170 hectares of the afforestation area is based on field inventory. The aims of this research is following in three stages: First stage: improve the accuracy and efficiency of individual tree crown delineation and detection, second stage: species determination of individual trees (instance segmentation); third stage: health status assessment of individual trees especially for declining and standing dead trees. It is predicted that the Mask R-CNN model will have acceptable performance in terms of accuracy and computational efficiency, providing a more effective tool for the management of the target forest and their environmental studies especially afforestation.
